  • The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are a set of internationally recognised standards published by the W3C. They ensure web content is perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust for all users, including those using assistive technologies.

  • Level AA is the widely accepted legal and practical baseline for most organisations. Level A is the minimum floor, and Level AAA is aspirational — targeting it where feasible benefits users with more complex needs but is not required for general conformance.

  • Unplug your mouse and navigate your page using only Tab, Shift+Tab, Enter, Space, and arrow keys. Every interactive element should be reachable, have a clearly visible focus indicator, and perform its expected action when activated by keyboard.
